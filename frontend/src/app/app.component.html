Question 5

If provided more time, what other data sources or techniques would you have used to provide insight for the MD?
In the present model for staffing levels, we only see a snapshot. Head counts presumably changed organically within each territory. If we had more information on how it changed over time since product launch in each territory that would improve the models. For example, it is possible that when entering a new market only direct sales works, and we need lots of marketing staff, and when market share increases then increasing retail sales staff and shrinking marketing team becomes necessary. Here domain knowledge is important, as it is difficult to say given a snapshot of markets at different stages of market penetration what the ideal level of staffing is on any given stage. Any additional data that helps explain market share would be also useful, such as marketing data, and country specific data (e.g. is it more widespread to do direct sales vs. retail sales).

I would have spent more time on Method 2 for Question 1 to validate the modelling approach, and use additional features to model the distribution of employees.

For both questions I would have spent more time on examining outliers and how/whether to deal with them. I would also spend more time on hyperparameter tuning for the models.

Based on the feedback I would provide additional views on the dashboards to investigate the data for business users.
